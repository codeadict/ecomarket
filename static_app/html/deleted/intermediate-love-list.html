Eco and Natural Credentials

Natural

The term Natural is often used to refer to products that have been produced using only unprocessed ingredients and which are free from artificial ingredients and additives. The marketing strategies for natural foods may be understood on two levels.

First: natural foods are consideredunprocessed foods in the sense that they are not influenced by industry for mass production.

Second: natural foods are seen as. Both dimensions are marketed as healthy for people and the environment.

Vegan

Vegans endeavour not to use or consume animal products of any kind. This means that Vegan products must, as far as is possible and practical, be entirely free from animal involvement.

Vegetable, mineral or plant/mineral-derived synthetic alternatives are used instead. Vegan products are free from animal testing.

Organic

Organic agriculture is a system of growing crops and rearing animals through natural processes and in natural conditions, with minimal artificial stimuli and disruption to the environment.

The use of pesticides, chemical fertilisers, insecticides, fungicides and routine antibiotics are either disallowed completely or they are restricted.

The Organic Trust was founded in Ireland in 1991 by a core group of dedicated organic producers including some of the pioneers of organic food production in Ireland. The Organic Trust offers a professional, user-friendly Organic Inspection and Certification Service for a vast range of organic enterprises including the full range of organic food. The scope of our certification covers the most prolific range of organic enterprises in Ireland at the present time. Currently this includes all organic livestock enterprises from organic beef, lamb and poultry units to the more specialised enterprises of organic dairy, pig, goat and deer production.
